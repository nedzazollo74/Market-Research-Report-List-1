<p><p>Aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage (aSAH) is diagnosed using various imaging techniques. Computed tomography (CT) scan is the first-line imaging tool, quickly identifying bleeding. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) offers detailed images of brain structures. Lumbar puncture is used for assessing cerebrospinal fluid, particularly in cases where CT is inconclusive. Xanthochromia indicates the presence of bilirubin in the fluid, signaling prior bleeding. Cerebral angiography visualizes blood vessels, revealing aneurysm details, while transcranial Doppler ultrasound evaluates blood flow and potential vessel spasms.</p></p>
