<p><p>The Electric Submersible Pump (ESP) market is segmented into low-temperature ESPs and high-temperature ESPs. Low-temperature ESPs are designed for operations in environments with fluid temperatures typically below 85°C, making them suitable for conventional oil and gas applications. In contrast, high-temperature ESPs operate in harsher conditions, handling fluids above 85°C, which is essential for enhanced oil recovery and geothermal applications. Each type is tailored for specific thermal conditions, ensuring optimal performance and efficiency in diverse extraction scenarios.</p></p>
